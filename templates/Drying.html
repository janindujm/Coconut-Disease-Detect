How to save my plant

  • Apply organic mulch around the base of the tree to retain soil moisture.

  • Address nutrient deficiencies with fertilizers: nitrogen (urea), potassium (muriate of potash), and magnesium sulfate.
  • Prune and remove severely affected leaves to prevent the spread of diseases or pests.

Reasons for drying of leaves

  • Prolonged drought or lack of sufficient water supply.
  • Soil nutrient deficiencies, particularly nitrogen, potassium, or magnesium.
  • Fungal infections, such as leaf blight or leaf spot diseases.
  • Pest infestations like mites, beetles, or caterpillars.
  • Improper soil conditions, such as poor drainage or low organic matter.

Precautions

  • Ensure consistent watering, especially during dry spells, to maintain moisture levels.
  • Use drip irrigation systems to minimize water wastage and provide steady hydration.
  • Apply balanced fertilizers periodically to meet the tree's nutrient needs.
  • Inspect trees regularly for pests or fungal infections and apply appropriate controls.
  • Enhance soil quality by incorporating organic compost and ensuring proper drainage.
