Yellowing of Coconut Leaves!!

Examples of Yellowing Coconut Leaves

How to save my plant

  • Apply urea (1 kg/tree/year) split into 2-3 doses for nitrogen deficiency.
  • Use muriate of potash (1.5 kg/tree/year) to address potassium deficiency.
  • Add magnesium sulfate (500 g/tree/year) for magnesium deficiency.
  • Ensure consistent watering, especially during dry periods (50 liters/tree every 3-5 days).
  • Enrich the soil with organic matter like compost or farmyard manure.

Reasons for Yellowing of leaves

  • Deficiency of essential nutrients like nitrogen, potassium, or magnesium.
  • Inconsistent watering leading to drought stress or waterlogging.
  • Pests like leaf-eating caterpillars or fungal infections.
  • Soil salinity, especially in coastal areas.
  • Poor soil health due to nutrient depletion or improper pH levels.

Precautions

  • Conduct regular soil tests to monitor nutrient levels and pH.
  • Maintain proper irrigation to avoid water stress or waterlogging.
  • Apply balanced fertilizers periodically to meet the tree's nutrient needs.
  • Use biological or chemical controls to prevent pests and fungal diseases.
  • Leach excess salts from the soil using fresh water in coastal areas.
